FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Xandar Kardian XK300.

The XK300 is a contactless vital sign monitor that uses impulse radio ultra-wideband (IR-UWB) radar to measure heart rate, respiratory rate, and movement without any physical contact. It mounts on a wall or ceiling and monitors through clothing and bedding.

The XK300 requires zero patient interaction — no cuffs, no finger clips, no wearables. This is critical for populations with dementia, agitation, or device-removal behaviors. The radar technology captures vitals continuously, 24/7, from up to 8 meters away.

The XK300 excels in night monitoring, fall detection, and continuous respiratory surveillance. Senior living communities use it for bed-exit alerts, apnea detection, and real-time respiratory rate tracking across Memory Care and Skilled Nursing units.

Yes. Through CCN Health, XK300 data flows into supported EHR systems including PointClickCare, ALIS, MatrixCare, and others. The integration provides continuous vital sign charting without manual documentation.

The XK300 monitors up to 8 meters with heart rate accuracy of ±2 bpm and respiratory rate accuracy of ±1 breath/min. It can detect micro-movements for fall risk assessment and provides presence/absence detection for elopement monitoring.
